Lauren's Story

Lauren was like any other child, going to the park, participating in sports, going to school and any other activities a 9 year old could think of. It all changed when she turned 10. She woke up in the hospital, with severe seizures, myoclonus, and the inability to walk. It took several months to finally be diagnosed with M.E.A.K., also known as, Myoclonic Epilepsy and Ataxia due to Potassium (K+). Lauren had a potassium related genetic disorder that usually expresses between the ages 3 to 15, and was among the 20 known cases in the world. As she aged she began to lose contact with the world, she lost many friends and went through periods of depression. Finally at age 24, she plucked up the courage to join a dance school. At first, even hand to hand contact would trigger myoclonic attacks, but despite the odds, Lauren flourished. She excelled quickly, and is a certified para dance athlete. She is an excellent artist, loves to give back to her community, and is a great artist.

About M.E.A.K

Myoclonus epilepsy and ataxia due to potassium channel mutation, also known M.E.A.K, is a genetic, neurological disorder that only has 20 documented cases. This disorder can cause individuals to suffer from seizures, slowed speech, and painful involuntary contraction of the muscles.
